Understanding climate chang Climate change refers to long‑term shifts in global temperatures and weather patterns, primarily driven by human activities. The widespread use of fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes has increased the concentration of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere, particularly carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide. These gases trap heat and cause the planet to warm, leading to significant environmental, social, and economic impacts. The effects of climate change are already being felt worldwide. Rising temperatures contribute to more frequent and intense heatwaves, floods, droughts, and storms. Sea levels are rising as glaciers and ice sheets melt, threatening coastal communities and critical infrastructure. Climate change also affects ecosystems, food production, and human health, often disproportionately impacting the most vulnerable populations. Addressing climate change requires both mitigation and adaptation. Mitigation focuses on reducing emissions through measures such as transitioning to renewable energy, improving energy efficiency, and changing consumption patterns. Adaptation involves adjusting systems and infrastructure to cope with existing and expected climate impacts, for example through flood defences, heat‑resilient buildings, and climate‑informed planning. Clear communication plays a vital role in climate action. Data, evidence, and policy proposals must be accessible and understandable to decision‑makers, organisations, and the public. Digital platforms like this site can support that process by making information more transparent and engaging.
